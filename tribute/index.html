    Here's a time line of Ted Nelson's Professional Life:

  • 1937 - Born in Chicago on June 17th.
  • 1959 - Earned B.A. in Philosophy from Swarthmore College.
  • 1960 - Began graduate work at Harvard.
  • 1960 - Founded Project Xanadu.
  • 1963 - Earned A.M. in Sociology.
  • 1964 - Began instructing Sociology at Vassar College.
  • 1966 - Ended instruction at Vassar.
  • 1966-67 - Worked at Harcourt Brace and Company.
  • 1967-69 - Consulted on the Hypertext Editing System and Files Retrieval System at Brown University.
  • 1968-69 - Classified work at Bell Labs.
  • 1968-69 - Created interavtice photo slideshows at CBS Labs.
  • 1973-76 - Staff position at University of Illinois at Chicago.
  • 1974 - Published Computer Lib / Dream Machines.
  • 1977 - Lectured in computing at Swarthmore College.
  • 1977 - Published The Home Computer Revolution.
  • 1980-81 - Worked as editor for Creative Computing.
  • 1981 - Published Literary Machines.
  • 1981-82 - Joined San Antonio, Texas-based Datapoint as chief software designer, remaining with the company as a media specialist and technical writer until its Asher Edelman-driven restructuring in 1984. .
  • 1988 - Autodesk acquired Xanadu technology.
  • 1993 - The Xanadu Operating Group is divested .
  • 1995-2008 - Teached Media and Information Science at Hokkaido University, Keio University, University of South Hampton, University of Nottingham, the Oxford Internet Institute and Wadham College in Oxford.
  • 2011 - Began work on ZigZag and XanaduSpace.
